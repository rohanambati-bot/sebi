Educational Resources: Spotting AI Fraud

          

            

              
1. Deepfake Executive Videos

              

                Deepfakes are generated by mapping an individual's facial coordinate mesh onto another body. In stock markets, deepfakes of CEOs announcing fake earnings or acquisitions are used for pump-and-dump manipulation. 
                
Forensic Signal: Notice face borders. Check if the face is softer (blurred) than the hair/background, or look for unnatural eye-blinking cycles.
              

            

            
            

              
2. Synthetic Voice / Call Cloning

              

                Voice cloning engines can replicate a person's vocal pitch, timbre, and cadence from just 15 seconds of audio. Fraudsters impersonate stock advisers in automated voice calls or voice memos to push urgent tips.
                
Forensic Signal: Synthesized voices lack room acoustics/noise. The background cuts to absolute dead silence between words because of generative sound boundaries.
              

            


            

              
3. Lookalike / Typosquatting URLs

              

                Phishing campaigns leverage close matches of broker or exchange domains (e.g. sebi-goviin.com instead of sebi.gov.in). They mimic authentic KYC portals to harvest OTPs.
                
Forensic Signal: Always double-check domain letters. Search our registry directly rather than clicking short links (like bit.ly) which hide the destination domain.
